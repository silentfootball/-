Origins & Overview
About the Game

Silent Football is a social game built entirely around restraint. An invisible ball moves between players using only codified whole-body gestures. Silence is the law during play — but when violations are called, punishments are proposed and spoken aloud to the Minister. Violations are judged. Punishments are carried out. Play continues.

The Premise

Players sit or stand in a group. The ball — invisible, weightless, and very real — is passed between them using whole-body gestures from the official Moves Registry. Anyone who speaks during play, makes a sound, or uses an unauthorized gesture has violated the rules.

The Committee

When a violation is spotted, play pauses and the Committee convenes. Any player may propose a punishment — spoken aloud to the Minister. The Minister hears all proposals and delivers the final verdict.

The Minister

One player holds the office of Minister. The Minister has absolute authority over all punishments. They may accept a proposed punishment, modify it, or impose an entirely independent verdict. There is no appeal.

How It Ends

The game has no set end. Play continues indefinitely until the Minister declares it over. There is no winning. There is no elimination — refusing a punishment results in being kicked out, but accepting it means you keep playing regardless of how many violations you've committed.

Official Regulations
The Rulebook
01
What Happens in the Game, Stays in the Game
Nothing that occurs during play is to be discussed, recorded, or shared outside of it. No exceptions. This is the highest rule.
02
Silence During Play
No verbal communication is permitted while the ball is in play. This includes words, whispers, sighs, laughter, and all other vocalizations. Silence is only required during active play — not during Committee proceedings.
03
The Ball is Real
There is exactly one ball. It is invisible. All players must treat it as physically present at all times. Disbelief is a violation.
04
Whole-Body Gestures Only
All moves must use whole-body gestures from the official Moves Registry. Unauthorized gestures constitute an immediate violation. The move list will be updated as new moves are approved by the Minister.
05
Calling a Violation
Any player may signal a violation by raising their hand in silence. Play pauses immediately. The Committee convenes and punishment proposals are spoken aloud to the Minister.
06
Committee Procedure
When the Committee convenes, any player may speak a punishment proposal aloud to the Minister. All proposals must be safe and non-harmful. The Minister hears all proposals before delivering a verdict.
07
Ministerial Authority
The Minister has final and absolute say over all punishments. They may accept a proposal, modify it, or impose an entirely independent verdict. No punishment that is physically harmful or dangerous may be given.
08
No Appeals
The Minister's verdict is final. Protesting a verdict — verbally or via gesture — is itself a violation and results in an additional punishment.
09
Accepting Punishment
All punishments must be accepted and carried out immediately. There is no elimination for violations — you carry out the punishment and keep playing. Refusing a punishment is the only way to be removed from the game.
10
No End Condition
The game has no fixed end point. Play continues until the Minister declares the game over. There is no winning or losing — only the Minister's authority and the ongoing rule of silence.
